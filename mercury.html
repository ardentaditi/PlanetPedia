MERCURY

Mercury is the smallest planet in our solar system and the closest to the Sun. It’s only slightly larger than Earth’s Moon. Mercury has no atmosphere to retain heat and experiences extreme temperature fluctuations.

Mercury has a large metallic core which makes up about 85% of the planet's radius. This core is surrounded by a silicate mantle and crust.

The surface of Mercury is heavily cratered and similar in appearance to Earth’s Moon, indicating that it has been geologically inactive for billions of years.
